Privacy

  • There's more than one types of privacy. And, those types are generally complex when it comes to online and protection
  • (generally) - privacy involves some sort of protection, but it's hard to form a single definition
    • Privacy can be lost or diminished
    • Privacy can be intruded on or invaded
    • Privacy can be violated or breached
  • Accessibility Privacy
    • Accessibility Privacy speaks to an individiual's freedom to physical non-intrusion
    • Focuses on harm that can be caused through phyiscal access to a person or their possessions
      • Being physically left alone
      • Being free from gov intrusion
      • Being free from other intrusion
    • Paporazzi
    • Doxxing
    • Google maps knows exactly where everybody is, and where their homes are
      • They have a responsibility to keep that private
    • As new tech develops the list goes on
      • IOT stuff
      • GPS data
      • Devices that "learn" your habits
      • Online advertising
      • Any data in the case of ransomware
  • Decisional Privacy
    • Speaks to an individual's freedom to non-interference of choice
    • Focuses on harm that can be caused through preventing access to making one's own decisions
    • Being left alone to make choices
      • Political
      • Social
      • Religious
      • Economic
      • Personal
    • Google has your data so they try to cater searches to fit your lifestyle, which is kinda weird
    • Censorship
    • Net Neutrality
      • IPS blocking text messages from certain organizations
    • Voter Suppression
      • Amazon reviews is kinda like a vote
    • ISP's favoring their own content above others
    • ISP's creating monopolies that don't compete
    • Censorship of legal content online
  • Informational Privacy
    • Information PRivacy speaks to an individual's freedom to information-protection
    • Focuses on harm that can be caused through having your data stolen or being locked out of your data
    • Protects against
      • Information theft
      • Blocking others from controlling the flow of their own information in any way
    • Becomes a more important issue as cybertechnology advances
      • In the past, paper records had to be discarded or destroyed to save physical space
      • Computers have an "infinite" amount of space
      • "Womb to Tomb" information
        • Before you're even born, to the time you die you'll information will be online
        • Causes problems for Facebook, Google, ICloud hack, Target hack, etc
  • Descriptive Privacy
    • What is the case
    • Natural Privacy
    • Walk by computer lab and observe someone using a computer
    • Loss of privacy (can't help it)
    • Example
      • A Teacher teaches a class, being in public, working in a public place
  • Normative Privacy
    • What should be the case
    • Violated Privacy
    • Stalk someone and spy on them using a computer at home
    • Violates expectations of privacy
    • Example
      • Your home, public bathroom
  • What you think it an offensive breach of privacy, I might think is fine
    • Therefore it's incredibly difficult to make laws when there is no clear line
  • Importance of privacy
    • Older generations tend to say "very"
    • My age and younger tend to say "not as much"
    • Western nations and urban areas say "very"
      • Individuals are seen as more important than the whole
      • This has changed a bit with security and political issues
    • Eastern nations and smaller communities say "not as much"
      • Broader community is seen as more important than individuals
      • Though, many individuals in society don't agree
    • Not everyone agrees
      • This makes it difficult to decide how it should be protected
  • Data Gathering and Exchange
    • Techniques are used to collect and record personal information usually not known to the user
      • Consensus data
      • Surveillance
      • Internet cookies
      • Shopping tracking (through "value" or "club" cards)
      • Cell-phone location tracking
    • That data is then shared between databases - in an effort to make data "more complete"
      • Advertisements
      • Traffic data
      • Business services
  • Data Merging
    • Combining data from multiple sources to identify individual records or groups of individua records
    • (DoubleClick)
  • Data Matching
    • Comparing data from multiple sources to search for matches or "hits" among those sources
    • (Super Bowl)
  • Data Mining
    • Combining and comparing data, then identifying non-obvious trends from that data
    • (credit risk)
  • Is there merit in data merginig, matching, or mining?
    • Probably yes
