  • Outsourcing:
    • When one company pays another company to do stuff/make stuff instead of doing those tasks itself
    • The US Outsourced 12% of all technology jobs in 2017
    • in 2007, 54% of tech CEO's questioned openly admitted to outsourcing a major part of their business
      • Now, 38% admit to it
    • Examples of Outsourcing
      • General Motors (us company) can outsource production of a car part to a German company
        • That German company can then outsource production of that part's components to another German company (those companaies manage their own internal business practices)
      • A California-based software company can outsource the manufacturing of computer components to a factory in China
        • That China-based comp oversees the hiring of its own employees in order to fulfill the order from California. The California company does not hire recruit or manage anything at the factory
      • Moral: Outsourcing doesn't have to be overseas, but it certainly can be.

    • By 2015, .......... it went up
    • In general offshoring is getting more popular
    • Examples
      • General Motors (a US company) opens a factory in China and manages internal production of a car part at that location.     • When a foreign company employs workers in a domestic location

Random GDBR Stuff

  • Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA):
    • Prohibits interception of email, or reading stored email, without a court order
    • Makes an exception for systems that a company owns, such as computers, cell phones, pagers, or other devices
    • Courts usually rule on the side of employers when they collect information from devices they own
    • So, if they own the device, they own some of your data, too
