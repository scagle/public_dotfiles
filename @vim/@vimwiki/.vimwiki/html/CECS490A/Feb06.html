Types of connections

  • Breadboard
    • Great for prototyping, not great for final products
  • Point-Point Soldering
    • Perfboard/Protoboard
    • VectorBoard
  • Through hole
    • Components that fit through a pcb and are soldered on the other side
  • Surface mounted devices
    • Useful and very small
  • Wirewrapping
    • Tight connections in small space, but a little effort
  • PCB - Printed circuit board
    • Final designs, expensive, but super solid/compact/professional

Circuit Board Design

  • In our projects, in our reports, we need a schematic
    • People in the past have tried LTSpice
      • This is more for simulation and modeling
      • For modeling circuits and verifying that they are going to work
    • PCB Creation Software
      • For documenting a schematic
      • For multiple engineers to look at the same documentation
      • For reports
      • For eventually creating a circuit board
      • Groups:
        • Hobbyist
          • Kicad
          • Eagle (used to be professional, now on it's way out)
          • EasyPCB123 (horrible according to eric hernandez
        • Professional
          • Orcad
            • Great
            • Older clunky interface
            • Libraries are going to have outdated parts
            • You might need to create components like tm4c1233gh6pm
            • You can import libraries, but:
              • because orcad libraries are professional, they have mostly industry parts
          • Mentor Graphics
            • Staple that has been around for so long
            • Ugly interface
            • Old engineers don't want to change
            • But backend is amazing
              • Can find a TON of issues in the pcb design
              • Has great algorithms to find problems
          • Altium
            • Easy interface
            • Easy to use
            • Pretty good library
            • Expensive
            • Kind've an Apple company where they don't listen to userbase
          • Altium/Circuit Maker
            • Circuit maker is the free version of Altium essentially
            • Pretty darn good
            • Has a couple caveats
              • Public domain designs, nothing is private
    • PCB Creation Steps
      • Schematic Entry
        • After schematic you take that design and turn it into a physical representation of your pcb
      • Layout + Routing
      • Generate Output
      • Gerbers
        • Gerber -> PCB fabrication house -> your home in 2 weeks as blank pcb
      • Soldering components to blank pcb
