  • How do we protect against copyright infringement
    • To even guess at an answer to this, we must take a look at one of the main laws that seeks to ........
    • 1976
      • Fair Use Doctrine
      • Attempts to easily tell whether some action violates copyright or not
        • Nonprofit vs Commercial use
          • Is what youre doing making money? or widely distributed?
        • Creative vs Factual material
          • Books
        • Amount and significance of content to be used
        • Effect on potential market value of original work
    • 1998
      • Digital Millennium copyright act
        • Protects against lawsuits by granting a short time period for web site
        • owners to remove copyright-violating material before a lawsuit can be filed.
    • 1984
      • Sony vs Universal Studios
      • Sony produced the first VCRs, which could record live television
      • People used the VCRs to record movies shown for free on television
      • Universal tried to sue Sony for enabling people to willfully infringe copyright, but lost
    • Significant Cases
      • Sega vs Accolade
        • Accolade stole Sega's stuff, Accolade went away
      • Sony vs Connectix
        • Connectix stole Sony's stuff, Connectix went away
      • Atari vs Nintendo
        • Nintendo stole Atari's system, then bought them before they sued them
        • Nintendo exploited policy vacuum, which is now closed because of them
      • Napster
        • Was the sharing of music via Napster fair use?
      • Napster's argument for fair use
        • The court's previously ruled that copying files for entertainment should be considered fair use
        • Didn't hurt corporate sales because their users sampled the music and then bough the album if they liked it
      • Industry's argument against fair use:
        • Trading and sharing with thousands of strangers should not be considered "personal use"
        • Songs and albums are creative works and users were copying entire albums (music, artwork, lyrics...)
        • Claimed Napster severely hurt music sales
