  • What is the European Union?
    • EU - General Data Protection Regulation - GDPR (2018)
      • Puts regulations on companies and organizations holding the personal data of any EU citizen
      • Applies regardless of the company/organization's location
      • All EU citizens must be able to control all of their data, and includes the right to be forgotten
      • Also known as the "EU Cookie Law"
      • Fines up to 20 million euros will be issued for non-compliance
    • EU - GAFA (since 2014)
      • Stands for Google, Amazon, Facebook, Apple
      • Where the US traditionally seeks to protect their largest companies, the EU and other regions take a more neutral stance on these types of companies
      • Involves tax issues, privacy violations and a general concern about EU data being held in the US
      • Currently the EU has more Internet users than the US (433 million vs 242 million)
  • How are US Companies Reacting?

    • And, the EU includes 28 countries. Blocking them all would be devastating to these companies
    • Some companies are just paying the hefty fines. But, that can't last forever

  • EU Internet Laws
    • Article 11 (2018)
      • Also called "The link tax" - a bit of a misnomer
      • Requires Search engines and other sites to pay creators for snippets of news content
      • But there's some confusion about what counts as a snippet
    • EU - Article 13 (2019)
      • Also called "The Meme Ban"
      • Says that content platforms will be responsible for all copyright violating content posted by users
      • Nobody's really sure how it will all play out, once the law goes into effect next year - and, each country can interpret it how they want
